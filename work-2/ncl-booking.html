Norwegian Cruise Lines

User Journeys, Concept Maps and Rethinking the Booking Experience

Overview

Norwegian Cruise Lines engaged IMC2 to help them rethink their entire digital consumer engagement an booking experience. While this project is from earlier in my career, I love this example as everything we worked on tied back to very detailed Target Audience Personas and Journey Maps that were developed to serve as a north star for the design work to follow.

In fact, IMC2 was called out by Forrester during this time for being an industry leading agency for our customer journey based design process.

Detailed Consumer Journey Maps
  • Robust user journeys helps us define big picture experience needs and communicate hem as stories.
  • Each step in the story keyed to specific features that should be built out to enhance the experience.

  • We often created "Concept Diagrams" to visually communicate at a high level how content and features would work and how they could evolve over multiple phases.

Shore Ex Booking Experience
  • Enhancing the Shore Excursion booking experience was a key assignment where we focused on not only simplifying the process but also merchandising and cross-sell / up-sell of the experiences.
  • The idea simply put was to facilitate in the overall planning activities (which are extensive for a cruise traveller)Â while motivating them to book as early as possible and generating excitement during the "waiting lull" prior to the trip.

F3 Booking screens
  • One particular challenge of designing the experience was working with the development team to understand the capabilities and constraints of the booking / reservation platform.
  • By understanding the platform, I was able to understand data calls and where to induce new kinds of data calls.
  • This allowed for the team to explore various ways to visualize things in various ways such as cabin availability and location.

F3 Future State Concept Wires
  • As part of the F3 rollout, the team was tasked with developing future state concept designs that would be part of a second phase build.
  • These screens leveraged heavily the idea of "cards" even though the notion of responsive layout and card or tile layouts was still far from mainstream.

F3 Content Map
  • One assignment included a complete new site for NCL's new flagship cruise liner that was soon to be launched.

    Part of this effort included wrangling all of the marketing content that could be used, identifying and how this could be repurposed, and identifying content gaps where new content needed to be produced.

    An effective way of communicating this was to develop large "site map" posters that were printed at large scale and used interactively.
