Sam’s Club

M-Commerce Design and Development (iPad and iPhone)

The Assignment

As Rockfish Interactive (now part of VMLY&R) served as Sam’s Club Digital AOR during my tenure, there were a number of large digital projects in flight. One of my favorite projects was to convert Sam’s Club e-commerce capabilities to a native mobile and tablet app experience for iPad and iPhone devices (Android was a fast follower). 

Although we worked in a very agile method with a lot of work sessions, white boarding and light documentation; below are some document examples that were created for key client reviews.

iPad screens with visual design and annotations.

There were overlap and similarities between handset and tablet experiences, but I took every opportunity to leverage each application based on the context in which it would be used.  
‍
The iPad had a more experiential and playful design solution versus the iPhone which had more focus on speed and utility.

iPhone screen flows with visual design and annotations.

In addition to many iterative design rounds, full detailed screen flows and final creative designs (with interaction notes) were provided to the development team.

iPhone screen flows with visual design and annotations.

Balsamiq was the wireframe tool of choice for this project since it's light weight and fast. It allowed me to quickly capture, document and refine what came from ideation and participatory design sessions.

I created screen flows that also showed screen states and behaviors based on how a user "type" would experience the site. From these baseline artifacts, I mocked up clickable prototypes that could be tested with real users - using lean (guerrilla) testing methods.
