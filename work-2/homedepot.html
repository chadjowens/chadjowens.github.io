Home Depot

Persona and Consumer Journey Maps

The Assignment

Home Depot engaged T3 to help them ideate and imaging a future state consumer journey experience for the brand as it plans to lean heavily into digital in the coming years.

Business Benefits

  1. Drive user engagement and commerce opportunities in new and innovative ways with the brand.
  2. Be seen in the marketplace as a digital innovator which provides a halo effect for overall brand perception.
  3. Better understand user need states and what will motivate consumers to interact with brand.

Process & Activities Overview 

  1. Extensive user interviews to uncover how each currently engages with the brand as well as where current unmet needs exist.
  2. We then developed key target consumer archetypes
  3. From key consumer archetype defined, we developed a journey map ideation framework
  4. The ideation framework allowed us to explore how cohesive digital and in-store experiences can be developed for each of the archetypes.
  5. Based on the future state experience journeys defined, each of the an opportunity summary was developed that detailed possible short term and long term initiatives.

Defining key consumer archetypes
  • As part of the research synthesis that occurred after the initial interviews, 3audience Persona / Archetypes were developed.
  • These were created to put clear focus and definition around the needs and motivations of a consumer group interested in a home decor / design offering.

Detailed consumer profiles
  • Personas created help define the following:
  • Goals, outlook, triggers that spark the motivation and common behaviors
  • Where they currently go for inspiration and ideas
  • Technographics (i.e. the channels and devices they are are comfortable engaging with) 
  • Degree of involvement in the process and perceived challenges
  • Shopping categories they have interest in as well as overall needs and wants

Future state journey maps
  • User Journeys help define the following:
  • The various stages of consumer engagement (i.e Trigger, Inspiration, Research, Interaction, Purchase, Post-purchase).
  • Within each stage, what the consumer was Thinking, Feeling and Doing was also considered - and if those events seemed positive or negative for the consumer.
  • Next, a conceptual narrative was developed that provided a framework for ideation around s and negative moments.
  • Each step within the narrative serves as a "moment" that details a channel opportunity with key near terms and future considerations.

Opportunity summary defined
  • The final summary details for each Persona / Archetype where digital and in-store enhancements to the consumer experience can be made.
