DISCOVER 
FINANCIAL SERVICES
Reimagining rewards and elevating the brand experience
Experience Director // Interaction Designer // CX Strategist
When I first joined FCB Chicago, I was immediately pulled into a large new business pitch effort for Discover Financial Services. I oversaw the effort from an Experience Design perspective and ran the creation of personas, a newly created information architecture scheme, interaction design and visual design recommendations.

After winning the pitch, Discover Financial Services quickly became a significant digital account for the agency and became my primary account focus for the next couple of years as the account grew.

While working with Discover Financial Services, I led over 20 projects that spanned multiple lines of business and digital touchpoints. Below are a few of the highlights

Streamlining Rewards

My first effort with DFS focused on rethinking how card holders experience the rewards program - Shop Discover. Simplifying the application process, streamlining the overall experience to support cross-sell opportunities and  making the account statement information easier to understand were also big considerations.
‍

Sample interaction design with detailed annotations

Detailed interaction designs were created that tied to core behavioral economics (and persuasion design) principals. These were outlined as key opportunities to differentiate the digital brand experience.
‍

Sample site architecture showing user flows

Part of the effort included recommendations of how to improve the overall site architecture. For these recommendations, I developed a new site map and navigation schema. I also used this to help orient the client on how the different personas would experience the site and based on their specific needs how they would likely navigate to reach their specific "Key Moments."

Mobile & Tablet Redesign

While working with Discover Financial Services, I had the opportunity to work with the mobile team and lead several projects to improve the mobile and tablet product experiences. The first project I worked on was a for a complete user interface overhaul and to introduce the Shop Discover rewards program to mobile and tablet. Part of this effort included a major audit to make sure desktop, tablet and handset experiences were starting to utilize the same design language.

iPad screens with visual design applied

Fully functioning clickable prototypes were created to pressure test functionality and the experience. The prototype was extensively tested with consumers . Joint Application Design (JAD) working session sketches

We began the engagement by inviting key business stakeholders to our offices for a half-day working session.  During this session, we reviewed key areas of stimulus such as Heuristics and Competitive Analysis. Through our Rapid Design Lab methodology, we reviewed numerous sketches that allowed us to quickly explore and discuss ways the UI could evolve. The intent of this exercise was to uncover areas of product and experience-based differentiation.

iPhone screens with annotations

Account Center Redesign

Another project that I really enjoyed while working with DFS was the concepting and redesign of the main cardholder account center experience. The purpose of this effort was to dramatically simplify how the account center experience worked and how cardholders get access to a very large amount of information in a simple, streamlined and elegant way. This effort included a lot of user research and testing to determine a final winning experience.

Account Center - expanded payment concept

A core concept of the redesign was to surface important functionality to the user without forcing them to leave the page. Additionally, we utilized the concept of Progressive Disclosure to provide users with clear paths to more detailed content contained within easily expandable content sections.

Manage account (multivariate test - bands vs tabs)

The overall redesign effort included user testing of various layout concepts. Within each of those broad concept categories, subtle variations inside each of the content container areas were tested as well.

Discover Lending Center

The Lending Platform assignment was designed to better integrate Discover’s credit management product offering into the DFS Account Center. Both Balance Transfers and Discover Personal Loans would be offered within the platform, along with tools and educational content.  

This was intended to provide customers with a single place to manage credit and educate them on their options. The integrated options would make borrowing more convenient and accessible to customers. It also enabled consumers to make more educated borrowing choices based on their personal circumstances.

WALMART
Improving store associate's lives through fun and measurable challenges
UX Lead // Interaction Designer
Walmart asked for a flexible application (My Sustainability Plan) as part of enhancement to their internal employee portal as a way to nudge employees to create and track meaningful personal goals.

The application would allow store associates to create and track goals that reflect personal lifestyle improvements they desired to make. Therefore, the application was geared heavily on the concepts of persuasion design. It utilized achievements, variable rewards and even competition to drive engagement. While keenly aware that the visual design of the screens are little dated, I really enjoyed this project due the "feel-good" nature of what the My Sustainability Project was attempting to accomplish and the complexity of the interaction design that was involved.

This tool became a core component of the main myWalmart.com intranet and was rolled out nationally as a beta test prior to developing multi-language extensions and launching globally.  

See More

Product screens with visual design applied

The visual design applied provided a light and airy feel to an experience that had a lot of backend functionality. User's were able to easily and intuitively understand how to navigate the application experience and take advantage of the features and functionality offered so set and track goals and share challenges with other colleagues within or across stores.
‍

Wireframes with annotations

The wireframe concepts were created with heavily documented interaction notes. This allowed the team to communicate complicated business logic and screen states to the client.

The application allows a user to create multiple goals, track goals with daily check-ins, gain achievements and unlock reward content.  Driving engagement through competition; by allowing associates to form teams and challenge other store locations, was another key feature.
