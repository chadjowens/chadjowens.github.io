Waste Management

Design Research Program

The Assignment

Waste Management engaged T3 to help them better understand a unique and somewhat nuanced market audience segment called Open Market Residential (OMR). This particular segment typically lives in the outskirts of major metro areas and has a choice of service providers.

The engagement included field research from 4 key market areas as well as a mobile ethnography study leveraging a platform called Over The Shoulder. This allowed us to get qualitative insights based on an in-home conversational approach (which is a snapshot in time) and more of a journal-based approach (which allows respondents a communicate over a longer time frame).

Our initial key insights from research validated two early truths that served as a cornerstone of the artifacts produced.

1) People are content when they know the COST of their service offering is on parity (within a reasonable range) with other competitors.
2) People associate VALUE of their waste management service relative to price in a variety of ways.

Based on these insights "Value Anchors" were a key element of how we framed each of the Archetypes and defined Experience Journeys for each of the Archetypes.

Program overview.
  • The Design Research program consisted of 3 aspects: Contextual Inquiry, Mobile Ethnography and a Quantitative Survey

Intranet trends assessment.
  • We transcribed every conversation from the field and captured every "utterance" in the form of small cards in our team war room.
  • Based on evaluating these utterances, we began to see patterns and form early themes. From there we established key insights that informed the creation of Archetypes and Journey Maps.

Field research & contextual inquiry.
  • Emerging themes and insights were part of an early post-field stories share out.
  • These were then refined into final key insights that were presented prior to developing Archetypes and Journey Maps.

Archetypes and mindsets
  • Consumer Archetypes were defined based on key insights and organized into consumers that were "Price conscious" with a secondary value driver.
  • The secondary "Value" drivers that informed consumer behaviors were:Â 
  • - Service (The Service Loyalist)
  • - Lifestage (The Overwhelmed)
  • - Trust (The Uninspired)
  • - Values (The Motivated)

Detailed consumer journey touch points
  • Journey Maps were broken into two seperate screens for each journey type:
  • - The "Journey Detail" which maps out Pain Points, Needs and Activities
  • - The "Opportunities" which details out (from a touchpoint perspective) opportunities that exist to improve consumer engagement for each of the defined Archetypes.
