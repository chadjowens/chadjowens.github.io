The Assignment

The Boston Consulting Group engaged T3 to help them explore the future of their internal employee experience. The assignment overall was focused on helping the organization rethink the experience of their global intranet platform and define ways their digital ecosystem of tools and resources could be improved to enhance productivity, connection and collaboration.

This engagement included extensive field research across their offices globally. We met with employees from the Boston, Atlanta, London offices physically and had remote interview sessions with employees from other various locations such as Munich, Germany and Gurgaon, India.

Program overview

The overall program consisted of:
  • Overview of existing tools, technology, reviewing relevant existing research, and conducting a trends and best practices analysis
  • Employee interviews (across a number of functional groups within the organization)
  • Insights creation that led to employee mindsets / modes, a high level journey map, experience principles, experience framework (system components) and concept moments
  • Strategic design recommendations for the most impactful "Key Moments" that would enhance the employee experience

    Sample: Employee mindsets and modes

    • One thing that became apparent throughout our research was there was less of a need to define distinct personas for this assignment and more value in defining employee "mindsets" based on different "modes" they may be in throughout their employee journey.

    Experience principles

    • As part of the synthesis phase we began to look for patterns that we then organized into emerging themes.
    • From emerging themes, we started to uncover clear insights. These 11 insights were then rolled up and organized into "Experience Principles" that served as guideposts for the following ideation and concept phases.

    Employee journey and system components

    • The artifacts created to help inform concept and ideation were more about minsets and modes. However, we discovered a critical issue within the organization was the rollout and adoption of new technologies or new ways of working.
    • This Adoption Journey helped to communicate that the rollout effort is just as important as the experience and design of the solution itself.

    Co-design work sessions

    • After key insights and initial concepting, we took exercises back into the field to further validate the priority of importance employees placed on "components" we defined based on insights.
    • The Co-Design work sessions dove deeper into exploring the type of content that was important to employees as well as the right tools and features that would best support their work and contribute to a more positive employee experience.

    Process & activities overview¬†

    1. Trends and competitive assessment
    2. Design research / contextual inquiry across multiple offices
    3. Synthesis to uncover emerging themes and establish key insights
    4. Full internal and client stakeholder team readout: field stories, emerging themes, experience principles and system components
    5. Co-design working sessions in Atlanta and London to validate and prioritize insights with card sort and concept sort exercises
    6. Final insights with key "moment" concepts recommendations brought to life
