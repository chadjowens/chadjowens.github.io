Texas Central Planning / Virgin Trains

Design Research Program

The Assignment

T3 was engaged by TCP/Virgin Trains to help them understand consumer needs, motivations and opportunity areas for a high speed rail service within the Texas Market. More specifically, a bullet train between Dallas and Houston.

We were assigned to take the Design Research insights and findings and create a vision of what the experience could look like as a means to excite the public and key constituents of the project.

We did 37 customer interviews across telephone, in-home and mobile "diary-based" ethnographies. We covered Dallas, Austin, Houston markets as well as explored the mindsets of travelers from New York, Washington DC, San Francisco and Denver.

Process & Activities OverviewÂ 

The exploration led us to 4 broad work product outputs.
  1. Traveler Mindsets and Modes
  2. Key Areas of Opportunity
  3. Experience Principles that drove concept ideation
  4. Schematic Experience Concepts and "Sizzle" Video that represented key signature moments in the traveler's journey

Program overview.
  • In-field contextual inquiry covered interviews with travelers from Dallas and Houston as well as, travelers who take the Acela Train from Washington DC to various cities along the North Eastern corridor.

Concept cards.
  • A concept card sort exercise was stimulus we included as part of the field research.
  • This exercise allowed travelers to react to conceptual illustrations of future state applications, services and experiences. It helped them imagine those coming to life and discuss how they would add value to their travel experience.
  • These concepts were then ranked in order of priority of value.

Traveler mindsets and modes.
  • Early emerging themes suggested that travelers often adopted "travel mindsets" which cut across the more demographic-based persona.
  • These travelers can also shift in and out of these various mindsets and modes within the same trip. For example, a traveller may be in a "business" mindset in the outbound direction of a trip and in more of a "leisure" mindset on the return leg of that same trip.

Key insights.
  • As part of our war room synthesis process, we transcribed each of the conversations and looked for common patterns.Â 
  • These patterns suggested themes that can then be further pressure tested and refined to become final insights.
  • The key insights served as guidelines for concepting around the following journey map and experience principle.

Micro-moment experience journey map.
  • Based on our initial understanding from existing research provided to us, we understood that the main traveler journey is 7 steps long.
  • However, even though the travelers agreed with our initial assessment they often had a perspective that added much more nuancedÂ micro-moments.
  • Overall, the journey map expanded to 11 steps and induced many detailed micro-moments that typically had negative impact on the traveler.

Experience principles.
  • The Experience Principles incorporate Key Insights as a way of structure guiding principles for ideation. These principles insure that the concepts created map back to strategic opportunities based on the real insights observed.

Concept descriptions.
  • The last exercise prior to developing schematic storyboards and a video was to develop a range of concept write-ups. These were based on both an internal brainstorm session as well as an all day Think Tank workshop with the client.
