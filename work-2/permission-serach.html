Permission.io

Rethinking the Search Experience

Overview

One of the last assignments I worked on at Drawn was with a small startup in Austin called Permission.io. The founder of Permssion.io had made a killing in partnership with Dr. Oz during the boom of the dot-com era on a digital product called Real Age. This start up is his attempt to make lightning strike twice by reinventing a niche search engine product for consumers that are savvy about crypto-currency and also have concerns about data privacy.

The Permission.io concept is a search engine that leverages blockchain and crypto-currency and will pay users for engaging with sponsored or contextually relevant ads based on their search query.

As this engagement was in fast paced start-up mode, the task was to help define the overall experience and quickly feed assets to the development team building the real world proof of concept.

Below are a few screens that highlight the platform.

Permission.io Search Landing
  • In this test and learn environment, the homepage was designed follow best-in-class design patterns based on major search engines and common learned behavior.

    The intent at this stage is to immediately funnel the user into the search flow and not distract them with any other content.

    However, links to additional information about the product and business are provided at the bottom of the screen.

Permission.io Search Results
  • Once the user sees their search results, they are also shown a prominent link to create an account or to view more information about how the platform works.

    Additionally, multiple sponsored ads could be displayed based in the search term and the user could earn by engaging.

    Based on our initial research the additional search categories that users wanted to view were broken out as tab navigation and follow a similar pattern to common popular search engines.

    The right-hand column was utilized to share other "higher-value" sponsored engagement opportunities  and other contextually relevant search result information.

Permission.io Search Results (Images)
  • The top of the screen would allow for content to be served dynamically based on the context and the user would also have the ability to select to view more.

    The intent was to A/B test the default state number of content elements to be displayed.

Permission.io Search Results (Shopping)
  • One of the driving concepts of the tool was to integrate with amazon or overstock API to drive engagement into shopping conversions.

    This fuels the ad network and the user gets the ASK crypto-currency for their engagement.
